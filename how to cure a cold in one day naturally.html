What Causes A Cold?

The common cold is named common for a good reason. There are over a billion cold worldwide each year. Occassionally, someone you know will be down with a cold. Colds are the most common reason that children miss school or parents being abcence for work.

Children are more prone to cold due to the weaker immune system that they have. Usually, cold are spread from children to children when they are in contact with other childrens during school hours.

Cold is very common and can occur at any time of the year, but they mostly happen during winter or rainy seasons.

A cold virus spreads through tiny, air droplets that are released when the sick person sneezes, cough, or blow their nose.

Usual ways of catching a cold:

  • A person with a cold sneezes, coughs or blow their nose near you.
  • You touch something that is contaminated by the virus and subsequently touched your nose, eye, or mouth.

The cold is the most contagious for the first 2 to 3 days. It would not be contagious after a week.

Most people do not understand why they got a cold but mainly the reason is caused by a low immune system. When you have a low immune system, you system is not able to fight off the virus initially. The virus gains control causing you to have a cold. The body then realise that the virus is getting control and your protective body system comes into action and eradicate the virus. Most colds go away in a few days.
